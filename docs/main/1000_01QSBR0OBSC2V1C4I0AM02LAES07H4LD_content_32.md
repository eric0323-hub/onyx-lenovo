## Symptom

* Touchpad is intermittently unresponsive.
* Touchpad does not work and a yellow exclamation mark appears on the device in Device Manager. It can automatically recover after a system restart.
  
  ![](https://ukm.lenovo.com/prod-api/file/download?key=accessory/8426d0b38c7f48bcaf160b572b31202a-1764237500842.png&name=image..png)

## Applicable Systems

* IdeaPad Slim5 14xxx8
  
  IdeaPad Slim5 14xxx9
  
  IdeaPad Slim5 16xxx8
  
  IdeaPad Slim5 16xxx9

## Solution

Update the Synaptics touchpad FW-004 to enable self-recovery for touchpad crash and optimized noise. Existing performance issues can be fixed. Windows Update (WU) is available from the end of April 2025.

Field solution:

You need to take the FFC cable out from the touchpad and cut the blue half-cut pull pad. Then put it back together again.

![](https://ukm.lenovo.com/prod-api/file/download?key=accessory/b3de21e8e84b40f78203abe89e64bc6b-1764559074591.png&name=image..png)

The blue pad of the touchpad FFC cable appears to be interfering with the black mylar pasted on the motherboard.

![](https://ukm.lenovo.com/prod-api/file/download?key=accessory/ce3e5ccb46584340b7b04407dcfe4eaf-1764559309105.png&name=image..png)

It has been noted that the length of the touchpad FFC pull pad is not suitable, as it is too long. This potentially causes a step with the black Mylar that could stress and loosen the FFC cable (especially with vibration). To resolve this issue, cut the blue pad in half and reassemble it correctly.
