### Description

The touchpad may become unresponsive or unable to click due to alignment in the C-Cover.

![100W Tchpd](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/100W TCHPD_20221101185520458.jpg)

### Applicable Brands

ideapad

### Applicable Systems

ideapad 100w Gen 3

### Solution

Do NOT replace any hardware for this issue.

1. Remove the base cover from the system.
2. Insert the blade end of the spudger under the metal retaining tab of the touchpad and rotate the spudger up to slightly bend the tab back.

![Spudger rotate](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514719/Spudger A_20230123162343449.jpg)

3. Reassemble the base cover and see if this adjustment addresses the issue.
4. If the issue still exists, remove the base cover and proceed with realigning the touchpad in the C-Cover using the steps below.

1. Disconnect the main battery from the motherboard.

![battery connector](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/Battery Connector_20221101190242571.jpg)

2. Disconnect and remove the ribbon cable from the sub-card and motherboard.

![subcard cable](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/Subcard Ribbon Cable_20221101185833514.jpg)

3. Remove the 4 screws holding the battery in and remove the main battery.

![battery](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/Battery Screws_20221101195253153.jpg)

4. Disconnect the ribbon cable from Touchpad and motherboard.

**NOTE:** When reinstalling, pay attention to the MB (motherboard) and TP (touchpad) markings on the cable!

![tp cable](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/Touchpad cable_20221101190749623.jpg)

5. Lift the mylar that goes along the top edge of the touchpad to expose the 3 retaining screws.

![mylar touchpad](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/Mylar touchpad_20221101191739820.jpg)

6. Remove the left and right retaining screws and loosen the center screw to adjust the touchpad alignment.

![tp screws](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/touchpad screws B_20221101192351494.jpg)

7. Make sure the screw bosses on the underside of the touchpad are centered in the plastic frame and tighten the center screw.

![screw boss plastic frame](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/screw boss_20221101192721888.jpg)

8. Check that the touchpad clicks freely and comes back to the correct position before tightening the other 2 screws.

NOTE: Open the unit slightly and press the touchpad to make sure it moves freely in both lower corners.

![test the touchpad](https://download.lenovo.com/km/media/images/HT514273/testing the touchpad_20221101194322718.jpg)

9. Once all 3 screws are tightened, fold the Mylar back down covering the screws before reassembling the system.
