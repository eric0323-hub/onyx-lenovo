### Description

This article is about how to maintain your PC.

### Solution

**1. Environment and running mode**

1. When using the computer, do not put the PC on your knee or any part of your body for a long time as this will restrict air flow and cause an overheating condition.
2. Protect your computer from liquids, especially water (to avoid the risk of electric shock and damage to the system).
3. Place the packing materials out of a child’s reach to avoid suffocation if the child plays with the plastic bag.
4. Protect your computer from extreme cold or extreme heat.

**2. Handle with care**

1. Never allow any object (including paper) between the screen and keyboard (or beneath) of your computer.
2. Do not drop, impact, scratch, twist, hit, vibrate, or push your computer, screen, or external device, or place heavy objects above them.
3. Do not stack laptops (do not use system while above another system).

**3. Carry and move the PC in a proper manner**

1. Before you move the computer, be sure to remove all media, turn off the connected devices, and disconnect the power cord and cables.
2. Before you move the computer, make sure that the computer is in standby or sleep mode, or turned off. This helps to prevent damage to the hard drive and data loss.
3. When picking up the computer in operation, use both hands with one on each side of the system. Do not grab or hold the screen only.
4. When transporting, do not place the computer in tightly stuffed boxes or bags.

**4. Set a password**

Please remember your password. If you forget the password for administrator or HDD, Lenovo will not reset these passwords. You may have to replace the system board or HDD.
