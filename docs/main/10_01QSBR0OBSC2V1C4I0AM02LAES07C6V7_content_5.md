Lenovo is committed to developing products and services that adhere to the highest security standards in order to protect our customers and their data. Lenovo welcomes information about potential product security vulnerabilities from security researchers, academics, and others in the wider security community.

Lenovo's Product Security Incident Response Team (PSIRT) is ready to work with those who bring such vulnerabilities to our attention and we strive to acknowledge all relevant submissions within two business days. We will investigate the issue, develop or source fixes, and then provide these fixes to Lenovo customers as quickly as possible. Lenovo may assign a CVE identifier per our [CVE Number Authority Information Sharing and Embargo Policy](https://support.lenovo.com/us/en/product_security/cna-policy).

Please review these instructions carefully to ensure security vulnerability findings are reported to the correct team within Lenovo. To ensure the best experience for you, we want to make sure you get to the right place for the issue you are reporting. The Lenovo PSIRT will not respond to the following types of reports and strongly encourages you to contact the responsible departments using the information provided here:

* Lenovo infrastructure and operations (including website/URL) issues should be reported directly to the Lenovo Security Response Center at [lsrc@lenovo.com](mailto:lsrc@lenovo.com)
* Motorola product security issues should be reported to [mbgpsirt@lenovo.com](mailto:mbgpsirt@lenovo.com)
* If you are reporting a lost or stolen product or need technical support, refer to the [Support Phone List](https://support.lenovo.com/supportphonelist) or link to our [Contact Us](https://pcsupport.lenovo.com/contactus) page
* For security vulnerabilities in the operating system (OS), Lenovo recommends that you contact the vendor of your operating system
* Non-security related AI trust concerns, such as user safety and well-being, bias, or misinformation involving Lenovo products or services, should be reported to the Lenovo AI Trust and Safety Center at [AITSC@lenovo.com](mailto:AITSC@lenovo.com "mailto:AITSC@lenovo.com")
  + At Lenovo, we are committed to responsible AI. We design and govern our AI to protect user rights, safeguard privacy, and promote fairness, transparency, and trust.

For product security vulnerabilities affecting Lenovo PCs, Servers, Tablets, Accessories, or AI Products send an e-mail to [psirt@lenovo.com](mailto:psirt@lenovo.com). You may use Lenovo's Product Security Incident Response Team PGP key to encrypt sensitive information (Click [here](https://download.lenovo.com/lenovo/content/psirt/lenovo_psirt_key.asc) to download our PGP public key).

Lenovo’s PSIRT only evaluates security vulnerabilities against the most current updates available at [support.lenovo.com](http://support.lenovo.com/) and recommends operating at the latest version of platform software, software applications and drivers (BIOS, BMC/TSM, FW, etc).

  

When reporting a product security vulnerability, you should include the following information:

* Name and version of the affected product or software
* Technical description of the issue and impact (include a proof of concept if possible)
* How to reproduce the issue
* How you discovered the vulnerability
* Public disclosure plans

  

Refer to the current Lenovo [Product Security Advisories](https://support.lenovo.com/product_security/home) site for information about the latest vulnerabilities.
