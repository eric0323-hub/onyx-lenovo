## Hardware

* New platforms – Intel Core Ultra (Panther Lake) & AMD Ryzen PRO (Gorgon Point/Hawk Point) with NPU → enables AI workloads, impacts diagnostics, thermals, and performance tuning.
  
  LPCAMM2 Memory (Intel) – new high-bandwidth, low-power module replacing SO-DIMM; single-module dual-channel, serviceable but requires strict torque & handling procedure
  
  5MP IR Camera (CV + vHDR) – advanced camera system; impacts troubleshooting (HW + driver dependency)
  
  PCIe Gen5 SSD – higher speed storage; thermal handling required during service

## BIOS/Firmware

* WLAN 6GHz Control (Regulatory) – BIOS + driver enforce region-based behavior; key for connectivity troubleshooting across GEOs
  
  Firmware Resiliency – auto-recovery from failed BIOS/EC updates; reduces no-boot scenarios
  
  BIOS Certificate Authentication – replaces password with certificate-based security; enterprise-focused
  
  Ultra Performance Mode – BIOS-controlled TDP increase; affects thermals, acoustics, and performance complaints
  
  Touch Panel Toggle (BIOS) – enable/disable touch at firmware level
  
  Power Button Reset Logic – 1s sleep / 10s shutdown / 14s reset; key troubleshooting shortcut

## OS/Software

* USB-C Diagnostic Integration – detects port damage vs system issue (UEFI + Vantage); avoids unnecessary FRU replacement
  
  Remote Management (Absolute / ThingsX) – remote wake (S4/S5), secure wipe, WWAN-based control; critical for enterprise servicing
  
  AI Noise Cancellation (Headset) – dual-mic algorithm isolates user voice; driver-dependent feature
  
  Lid Closed Performance Boost – +TDP when docked (AC required); impacts thermals/noise troubleshooting

## Mechanical (Service Repair)

* Cleansheet CRU-first design – easy bottom cover removal with captive screws and clearly marked components. Toolless internal battery – snap-fit, cable-free design
  
  Replaceable USB-C Modules – no system board replacement needed; includes damage detection (HW + SW diagnostics), requires correct install order (USB1 → USB2)
  
  Wi-Fi 7 + WWAN (4G/5G) – higher throughput; complex antenna routing under battery increases service sensitivity
  
  Top-load keyboard with replaceable keycaps – simplified servicing with reduced need for full keyboard replacement
  
  CAMM memory handling – tight removal and strict torque sequence required; improper handling may damage connector or module
  
  Overlapping Type-C modules – incorrect installation order causes mechanical conflict between connectors
  
  LCD service limitations – bezel is non-reusable and strong adhesive may damage panel during removal
  
  Complex module access – NFC module and cable folding design make removal and reassembly difficult
  
  Critical cable routing near hinge – incorrect routing of LCD/camera cables may cause installation issues or connector failure
  
  Backup screws – additional screws exist but are not labeled, requiring reference to documentation
  
  Check the FRU removal/replacement videos [here](https://support.lenovo.com/us/en/solutions/ht505031).
